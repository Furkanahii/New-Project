Nadia Murad

From Survival to Nobel Peace Prize: A Voice for the Voiceless

Updated 2024 By Sarah Ahmed 20 min read

Nadia Murad's journey from a survivor of genocide to a Nobel Peace Prize laureate exemplifies extraordinary courage and resilience. Her advocacy has brought global attention to the plight of survivors of conflict-related sexual violence and the Yazidi people.

"I want to be the last girl in the world with a story like mine."
— Nadia Murad

Early Life and Survival

Born in Kocho, Iraq, Nadia Murad grew up in a close-knit Yazidi community. Her life changed dramatically in 2014 when ISIS attacked her village, leading to the genocide of the Yazidi people. After surviving unimaginable horrors, she escaped and transformed her pain into a powerful voice for change.
