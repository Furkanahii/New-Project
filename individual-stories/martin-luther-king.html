Martin Luther King Jr.

The Dream That Changed America

Updated 2024 By James Anderson 25 min read

Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.'s leadership in the American civil rights movement transformed the nation's pursuit of racial justice through nonviolent resistance and unwavering moral courage. His vision of equality and human dignity continues to inspire movements for social justice worldwide.

"I have a dream that my four little children will one day live in a nation where they will not be judged by the color of their skin but by the content of their character."
— Martin Luther King Jr., August 28, 1963

Early Life and Education

Born on January 15, 1929, in Atlanta, Georgia, Martin Luther King Jr. grew up in a family deeply rooted in the African-American Baptist church and the fight for racial equality. His father, Martin Luther King Sr., was a prominent Baptist preacher and civil rights leader in Atlanta.

The March on Washington

On August 28, 1963, King delivered his famous "I Have a Dream" speech at the Lincoln Memorial, addressing over 250,000 people. This speech became a defining moment in the civil rights movement and American history.
